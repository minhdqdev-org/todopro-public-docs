# Sharing and collaboration

Share a project to work on it with other people.
Everyone in a shared project sees the same tasks, and changes appear live for all of them.

!!! info "Pro feature"
    This requires a TodoPro Pro subscription. See [TodoPro Pro](../pro/index.md).

## Invite someone to a project

=== "Web"

    1. Open the project and go to its members.
    2. Enter the person's **email address**. TodoPro autocompletes existing TodoPro users as you type.
    3. Choose the role they should have.
    4. Send the invitation.

=== "Mobile"

    1. Open the project and open the members sheet.
    2. Enter the email address; matching TodoPro users are suggested as you type.
    3. Choose a role and send the invitation.

The person receives an invitation they can accept or decline.
Until they accept, they appear in the members list as pending.

!!! tip
    Inviting someone who does not have a TodoPro account yet still works — they are invited by email and join the project once they sign up and accept.

## Accept or decline an invitation

You can respond to an invitation in two places:

- From the **invitation link** you were sent.
- From your **notifications** inside TodoPro, on web or mobile.

Accepting adds the project to your sidebar.
Declining leaves everything as it was, and the person who invited you can invite you again later.

## Roles and permissions

Each collaborator has a role that decides what they may do in the project.
The **owner** has full control, including deleting the project and transferring ownership.
Administrators can manage the project and its members.
Other roles are limited to working with tasks.

Project actions are gated by role: if your role does not allow an action, the option is not available to you.

## Change a role

Open the members list, find the person, and change their role.
The change takes effect immediately.

## Remove a member

Remove someone from the members list to revoke their access to the project.
Their existing comments and activity remain in the project history.

## Revoke a pending invitation

If an invitation has not been accepted yet, you can revoke it from the members list.
The link stops working.

## Leave a project

If you are a member rather than the owner, you can **leave** a project you no longer need.
It disappears from your sidebar and you lose access to it.

!!! warning
    The owner cannot simply leave a project. Transfer ownership to someone else first, or delete the project.

## Transfer ownership

The owner can transfer project ownership to another member.
After the transfer, the new owner has full control and the previous owner stays on as a member.

Use this when you hand a piece of work over or before you leave a team.

## Assign tasks

In a shared project, a task can be **assigned** to a member.
Assigning makes it clear who is responsible, and the assignee can see what is on their plate.

Open a task and set its assignee from the task detail view, on web or mobile.

## Comments

Every task has a comment thread.
Use comments to discuss the work where the work lives, instead of splitting the conversation into chat and email.

## Attachments

Files can be attached to tasks and to comments.
Images preview in the app; other file types open in the app you normally use for them.

Attachments are visible to everyone who can see the project.

## Project activity log

A shared project keeps an activity log of what changed and who changed it.
Individual tasks also keep their own history.

Use it to catch up after time away, or to check when something was moved or completed.

## Real-time updates

Changes made by anyone in a shared project appear on everyone else's devices right away — no refresh needed.
This works across web, mobile, and the CLI, since they all share one account.

If a device is offline, its changes are queued and applied when it reconnects.
See [Apps](../apps/index.md) for how each surface handles offline work.
