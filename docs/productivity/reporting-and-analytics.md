# Reporting and analytics

TodoPro records what you complete and when, and turns that into reports you can act on. The web app has a dedicated **Reporting** page; the mobile app shows the same kind of information as charts.

## Opening Reporting

=== "Web"

    Choose **Reporting** in the sidebar. Pick the period you want to look at, and the charts update.

=== "Mobile"

    Open the reporting screen from the app's navigation to see your charts.

=== "CLI"

    ```bash
    todopro analytics
    ```

## What you can see

### Completion statistics

How many tasks you completed over the selected period, and how that compares to how many you created. This is the headline number: are you closing more than you open?

### Productivity trends

Your completion volume plotted over time, so you can see whether you are speeding up, slowing down, or holding steady.

### Per-project statistics

A breakdown by project. Useful for spotting the project that quietly absorbs most of your week, and the one you keep meaning to get back to.

### Completion patterns

When during the day you actually finish work. If most of your completions land in a two-hour window, that window is your real productive time — protect it and schedule your hardest tasks there.

### Overdue analysis

A look at tasks that passed their due date: how many, and how they build up. Persistent overdue counts usually mean you are dating tasks optimistically rather than working too slowly.

### Streaks

Consecutive days on which you completed work. Streaks reward consistency rather than volume.

### Label usage

Which [labels](../tasks/labels-and-filters.md) you use most. A good way to notice that a label has stopped being meaningful, or that one category of work is dominating.

### Period comparison

Compare one period against the previous one — this week against last week, this month against last month — to see the change rather than just the total.

### Weekly summary

A digest of the week: what you finished and how the week looked overall. A natural companion to a weekly pass through the [Review](today-and-upcoming.md#review) view.

### Custom reports

Build a report around the criteria you care about, instead of relying only on the standard views.

## Exporting your analytics

Analytics can be exported separately from the rest of your data, so you can keep your own records or work with them in a spreadsheet.

!!! note
    Exporting analytics is different from exporting your account data. To take your tasks and projects with you, see [Import and export](../account/import-export.md).

## Reading the numbers well

- **Compare, do not judge.** A single low day means very little. Use period comparison and trends.
- **Follow overdue analysis to a cause.** It is almost always a scheduling habit, not an effort problem.
- **Let completion patterns change your calendar.** Move demanding work into the hours where you already finish things.
